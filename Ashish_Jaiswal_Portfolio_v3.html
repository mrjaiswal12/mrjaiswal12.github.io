AI equity portfolio — Markowitz optimisation & ESG strategy
Dec 2024 – Apr 2025  ·  $100K simulated portfolio  ·  16 trades
Completed
Built a simulated $100K portfolio from scratch using Markowitz Mean-Variance Optimisation. The actual work was less glamorous than it sounds — a lot of time spent on data cleaning, testing assumptions, and figuring out why the model kept blowing up on certain inputs. Stock selection was driven by top-down macro analysis: Fed rate trajectory, US inflation expectations, and geopolitical risk positioning. Each position was valued independently using DCF and relative valuation before entering the portfolio. The final result: 6.60% return against the S&P 500's -1.92% over the same period. Sharpe of 7.60, beta of 0.716 — deliberately lower volatility, higher risk-adjusted return.

MSc dissertation — ESG performance & government equity ownership
University of Southampton · 2024–2025
Academic
Spent six months working through a panel dataset of 11,000+ firm-year observations across global industries to test whether government equity ownership moderates the ESG valuation premium. The short answer: yes, it does — but the direction depends on context. Fixed effects model, multiple robustness checks, and more iterations on the variable construction than I'd like to admit. The research confirmed a positive, statistically significant relationship between ESG performance and firm valuation.
